Chhole Bhature

By User

Chhole Bhature is a delicious and hearty dish that is often enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner in North India. The spicy and tangy chickpea curry pairs perfectly with the soft, fluffy, and deep-fried bhature, creating a delightful combination of flavors and textures.

Ingredients:

For the Chhole (Chickpea Curry):

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas (or 2 cans of chickpeas)
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 5-6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 teaspoons cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Water
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

For the Bhature (Fried Bread):

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Water for kneading
  • Cooking oil for frying

Instructions:

For the Chhole (Chickpea Curry):

  1. If using dried chickpeas, soak them in water overnight. If using canned chickpeas, rinse and drain them.
  2. In a pressure cooker or a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons of cooking oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  4. Stir in minced ginger and garlic and cook for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil starts to separate.
  6. Add all the dry spices – coriander powder, cumin powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook the spices for a minute or two.
  7. Add the soaked (and drained) chickpeas and mix well.
  8. Add enough water to cover the chickpeas and bring it to a boil. If using a pressure cooker, cover and cook for 2-3 whistles, or simmer in a regular pot for 45-60 minutes until the chickpeas are soft and cooked.
  9. Once the chickpeas are cooked, add garam masala and simmer for an additional 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

For the Bhature (Fried Bread):

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, semolina, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, and salt.
  2. Add yogurt and 2 tablespoons of cooking oil. Mix well.
  3. Gradually add water and knead the dough until it's smooth and pliable. The dough should be soft but not sticky. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 2 hours.
  4. After resting, divide the dough into small portions and roll them into balls.
  5. Heat cooking oil in a deep pan for frying. It should be hot but not smoking.
  6. Roll each ball into a circle or oval shape (bhatura) and gently slide it into the hot oil.
  7. Fry each bhatura until it puffs up and turns golden brown on both sides. Press it down gently with a spatula to help it puff up.
  8. Remove the bhatura from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

Serve the hot Chhole with the freshly fried Bhature. You can also serve it with sliced onions, pickles, and yogurt for a complete and satisfying meal. Enjoy your homemade Chhole Bhature!
